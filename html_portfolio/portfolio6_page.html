    Comparative Study On Different Types of Computers

    Introduction

    Computers are defined to be electronic devices that can be programmed to accept, process, and store data according to a specific set of instructions. It performs a wide range of tasks that make everyday tasks more convenient and easier. Through the development of these computers, the world was able to connect despite being islands away from each other.

    Looking back, computers were not the way they were before. It started from Engines, specifically, the Analytical Engine by Charles Babbage way back in the 1830s when its purpose was purely to make mathematical calculations. Over the years, it slowly grew to become more programmable, more modular, and more dynamic in terms of functions and purpose until such time that everybody had to have one computer system.

    As technology continues to progress, computers get more and more better but it also gets a lot more expensive. For this reason, knowing what type of computer to use is essential in the modern way of living as it has become essential in most, if not all companies in the world. Therefore, knowing how the different parts of the system contribute to the performance of a computer will decide, and find the most suitable computer type for an individual’s needs.

    This paper will discuss all the different types of computers, going in-depth into their components, their processing speeds, energy consumption, and where these computers are used in society in such a way that their full potential can be drawn out. Serves as a guide to what the different types of computers are capable of and in which places they fall short.

    Discussion

    To fully understand to which extent these computers can perform and what their capabilities are, we need to know how they differ in terms of components, parts, performance, and functionality. However, before that, we must first know the main types of these computers, which this section will cover.

    Types of Computers (based on size)

    • SuperComputers

      considered to be the most powerful computers. It is capable of performing an estimate of trillions of calculations per second. These computers are utilized in the field of science, which incorporates complex computations such as weather forecasting.

    • Mainframe Computers

      These are large-scale computers capable of handling vast amounts of data and supporting hundreds of users simultaneously. It is commonly used for bulk data processing in large organizations

    • Minicomputers

      Known as mid-range computers. Smaller than mainframes but larger than personal computers. Used for tasks that require multiple users such as industrial applications.

    • Workstations

      These are high-performance computers designed for technical or scientific tasks. They possess enhanced graphics capabilities and are commonly used in engineering and animation.

    • Personal Computers

      General-purpose computers designed for individual use. Includes desktops, laptops, and netbooks.

    • Microcomputers

      A subset of personal computers that use a single microprocessor.

    • Servers

      These are high-performance computers, which provide services to another computer over a network.

    Comparing these types of computers boils down to what their components and characteristics are and how those specific components and characteristics contribute to their performance. In this section, we specify some of these characteristics as the following:

    1. CPU

      The performance of the CPU is based on their clock speed, core count, and, architecture. Higher clock speeds mean the CPU can perform more cycles per second. Multiple cores allow parallel processing improving multi-threading.

    2. Memory

      Larger amounts of memory allow more applications to run simultaneously without slowing down the system.

    3. Processing Speed

      refers to how quickly a computer can execute instructions. Determined by clock speeds and the efficiency of their architecture.

    4. Calcuting Power

      a computer's ability to perform complex mathematical operations efficiently. This is often measured in Floating-point operations per second (FLOPS) or Million instructions per second (MIPS), which generally indicates how many floating-point arithmetic calculations can a computer complete in one second.

    5. Working Principle

      involves fetching instructions from memory, decoding them, executing operations via the CPU, and storing them back in memory or storage.

    6. Energy Consumption

      More powerful computers often consume more energy due to their high-performance components. Maintaining low power consumption while maintaining performance is a challenge for high-performance computers.

    7. Field of Use

      determines which type of computer is most suitable for specific applications. Understanding this helps in selecting the right type of computer for different applications.

    In the context of CPUs, there is such thing we call “Processor Architectures”, they are fundamental designs that define how computers operate and interact with other components. It determines the instruction set, data processing capabilities, and overall performance of the processor.

    Types of Processor Architecture

    RISC(Reduced Instruction Set Computer)

  • Designed for efficiency, enabling higher performance with lower power consumption.
  • Ideal for mobile devices
  • Example: ARM(Advance Risc Machines) processor

    • C(Complex Instruction Set Computer)

  • Designed for more powerful processing capabilities, with generally higher power consumption as it requires more cycles to execute instructions.
  • Example: x86 Architecture
